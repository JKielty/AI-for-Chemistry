# Coursework Brief: Predicting Materials Properties with Machine Learning

## Overview

In this coursework, you will work with a real materials science dataset containing computed refractive indices for 4,764 materials, derived from Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations. Each of you will receive a **unique, randomly subsampled and mutated dataset variant**. This means your data will differ slightly from your peers’, so **do not expect identical answers or models**.

The refractive index is a key optical property affecting how materials interact with light. Predicting or designing materials with tailored refractive indices is crucial in applications such as:

- Photonic devices (e.g., waveguides, optical fibers)  
- Solar energy harvesting and coatings  
- Sensors and imaging systems  

## Objectives

Your goal is to apply the principles learned in the course to develop a machine learning model that predicts the refractive index of unseen materials from the dataset. You may use any of the types of models covered in the course, or, if well motivated, other models and methods.

## Deliverables

1. A notebook (or notebooks) containing your full workflow, including data loading, preprocessing, modeling, and evaluation. With each step cleaely explained and design decisions justified.
2. A brief written critique (approximately 300-500 words) discussing:

   - Which model you found preferable and why  
   - The strengths and weaknesses of your chosen model  
   - Potential improvements or future directions to address current limitations
  
### Notes for Students:

- Be sure to support your critique with specific examples or reasoning based on your model experiments.
- Reflect on how your model choices would affect real-world applications.
- Consider how the source and size of your data influence model reliability.
- Think broadly about the impact of your modeling work.
